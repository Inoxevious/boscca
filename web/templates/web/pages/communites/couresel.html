Botswana Savings and Credit Cooperative Association (BOSCCA) was registered on the 27 th November 1998, with the main objective of disseminating information pertaining to Savings and Credit Cooperative Societies (SACCOS), and providing sound operational methods and practices relevant to all concepts of present and future SACCOS.

BOSCCA Objectives

The Association was also to stand as a legitimate apex organisation, representing SACCOS at national and international forums, as a collective national and international voice for the SACCOS movement in Botswana. Learn more arrow_forward

To identify, promote, organize and develop SACCOs in Botswana;

To improve access to financial services to the vast majority of Batswana who lack access to affordable and readily available financial services;

To revive all dormant communal/institutional savings and credit societies and encourage the potential and viable ones to further develop and attain self- sufficiency;

To disseminate information pertaining to SACCOs and provide sound operational methods and practices relevant to all concepts of present and future SACCOs;

To protect members funds and life against any possible loss. To establish a central finance fund whereby SACCOs will pool their resources together to create a source of lending to Societies;

To build and maintain a financially sound network of Savings and Credit Co- operatives to mobilize and effectively channel savings to help meet the social and economic development needs of its members and the community in general;

To foster the vitality of SACCOs to become effective financial intermediaries by providing programmes and services that will enhance the SACCOs ability to successfully meet the needs and expectations of their members;
